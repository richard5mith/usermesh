Friday, February 15th 2013, 5:40pm

MG Siegler wrote a post talking about your next games console being made by Apple. Just like I did last week.

He criticises Microsoft for not updating the Xbox in 7 years, which I think is unfair. That's not the way the console industry has traditionally worked. You take a bath on the initial price of the hardware because you hope to make that app in game sales later, which is why they're able to make them so powerful, and why the hardcore want to buy one - because the games look cool. A shorter cycle means you sell less games and have less time to make back that initial investment.

The same goes for developers, they don't want a short cycle either. Then you're always having to chase the next release, and you never get a stable platform to work on. One of the benefits of consoles is that it's nice and simple to tell what everyone has, without the fragmentation. More hardware updates just means publishers don't want to take the risk on funding $100 million triple AAA titles for a small segment of the market with consoles powerful enough to play them.

There is no doubt that there's a future for Apple, or an Ouya or whatever in the living room. But comparing them to Sony or Microsoft is unfair. It looks like they're in the same business, but that's what Nintendo thought with the Wii too, and look what happened to their attempt to just go after the mainstream - a whole lot of consoles in cupboards and nobody buying anything.

Superbowl 2013 Ad Picks

Monday, February 4th 2013, 9:48pm

The superbowl was yesterday, and that means it was once again time for advertising executives to make us ashamed that they consider these the best way to get their message across.

This one makes no sense at all. They barely show the phone, they show a bunch of things it obviously can't do (with the suggestion at the end that somehow it can set you on fire and then put it out again...?) and do absolutely nothing to tell the viewing public why they should suddenly care about Blackberry again.

The Volkswagen one is borderline racist, but it's mostly just a very peculiar way to advertise a car.

The Audi one on the other hand is just a genuinely good ad.

This is the Sodastream ad they weren't allowed to run because it made fun of Coke and Pepsi, two of TV's biggest advertisers...

And this one is pretty fun, and you can tell most of it was made up when Rudd is trying to stop himself laughing...

There were also new spots for Star Trek: Into Darkness, Iron Man 3, The Lone Ranger and World War Z. But the only one I want to mention is Fast and Furious 6. The last one was just insane, and I think most people are probably saying "really? another one". But you have to admit from this spot, they're really just going nuts now. Pulling down a plane from takeoff, bursting a car out the front, and then somehow they're going to explain how they can bring a previously killed character back from the dead? Genius.

The rest? Instantly forgettable, overly expensive drivel.
